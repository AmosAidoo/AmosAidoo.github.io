I once stumbled upon an article back in 2014 talking about how cool one can do cool stuff with notepad and command prompt. It really caught my attention and I decided to learn more about it. It was about writing windows `.bat` batch scripts to run on command prompt. I wasn't really understanding what was going on so what I used to do was to memorize some scripts from the internet and show them to some of my friends. My favourite was random characters falling with a green background(The Matrix Effect). In that same year, I was preparing to write my [B.E.C.E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_Education_Certificate_Examination#:~:text=The%20Basic%20Education%20Certificate%20Examination,of%20junior%20high%20school%20education.) exam I gave it a pause to focus on the exam.

After the exam, I decided to go back to the batch scripting. I didn't have a laptop by then so I used my brother's laptop. I read through some scripts on the internet and typed out most of them to see the outcome, then one day things started making sense to me. `if..else` statements were used to make decisions, `:labels` were used to mark portions of scripts, `goto` statements were used to move to those portions and `variables` were used to store values. I was really excited about that. I could finally read scripts from online and understand and I could write scripts for what things I thought about.

In that same year(2014), I had admission to [Mfantsipim School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mfantsipim_School). There was a computer club over there so I joined the club so that I could learn more. The truth is most of us didn't really know much. Little by little,I, together with the friends I made, started to learn some HTML and CSS. I also taught some of them the batch scripting that I knew. I learnt alot of things from them too. I tried to learn Javascript but I was so confusing back then that I gave up on it and focused on doing just HTML and CSS which I got extremely good at. Aside Javacript, I attempted C++ and Processing but I still wasn't understanding things. Concepts like `for loops`, `functions`, `data types`, e.t.c were really confusing to me.

In 2017, I wrote the [W.A.S.S.C.E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_African_Senior_School_Certificate_Examination) exam and completed my secondary school education. Whilst waiting for the results, I continued with HTML and CSS, and built alot of websites with them (all of which I have unfortunately lost). Along the way, I wanted to learn about Android Development. I found out that I needed to learn Java first so I gave Java a try. I searched for some articles on Java, read through them but the `Hello World` program for Java  found in all these articles were 'complicated'. `Class` here, `public static void` there. So I quit once again. Some weeks later, I came back to Java but this time I decided to watch a video instead of reading. I found a 5 hour long video ([link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R08YRplsYGw)) and patiently walked through it. Everything finally made sense to me, wow. I continued practicing Java(though I never really did Android Development). Fortunately, I passed my W.A.S.S.C.E and got the opportunity to study Computer Science at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology. Our first programming course was just like what I had done in Java about 2 months ago so I didn't struggle at all. This gave me the peace of mind to explore other things. Without any specific goal in mind, I let my curiousty lead me to study about alot of things and pick up alot of skills. I met great friends who have also helped me stay focused on my studies. From all the things I studied, in November 2020, I finally decided that it would be great to specialize in backend development and I am looking forward to using this blog of mine as an opportunity to learn and share my knowledge.

Thank you for reading and welcome on board.
